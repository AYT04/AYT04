## Roots:

The roots of a quadratic equation are the values of the variable that satisfy the equation. These roots can be found using various methods, including factoring, completing the square, and using the quadratic formula. The sum of the roots of a quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ is given by $-\frac{b}{a}$ and the product of the roots is given by $\frac{c}{a}$ 

The nature of the roots can be real or imaginary, and if the coefficients are real, the roots will either be both real or a pair of complex conjugates  If the quadratic equation has rational coefficients, any irrational or imaginary roots will occur in conjugate pairs 

For a quadratic equation with roots $\alpha$ and $\beta$, the equation can be written as $a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) = 0$ where $a$ is a non-zero constant

The roots of a quadratic equation can be calculated using the quadratic formula:
$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

The discriminant $b^2 - 4ac$ determines the nature of the roots:
- If $b^2 - 4ac > 0$, there are two distinct real roots.
- If $b^2 - 4ac = 0$, there is one real root (a double root).
- If $b^2 - 4ac < 0$, there are two complex conjugate roots

## Axis of Symmetry

The axis of symmetry in quadratic relations is a vertical line that divides a parabola into two equal halves, making them mirror images of each other This line always passes through the vertex of the parabola For a quadratic equation in standard form, $y = ax^2 + bx + c$, the axis of symmetry can be found using the formula $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$ When the quadratic equation is in vertex form, $y = a(x-h)^2 + k$, the axis of symmetry is $x = h$ The axis of symmetry helps determine the shape and orientation of the parabola.

## Discriminant

+ If the discriminant is positive, there are two distinct real roots.
+ If the discriminant is zero, there is exactly one real root (a double root).
+ If the discriminant is negative, there are two complex conjugate roots.

## Factoring

Factoring quadratic equations involves expressing the polynomial as a product of its linear factors. For a quadratic equation in the form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, the goal is to rewrite it as $(x - k)(x - h)$, where $h$ and $k$ are the roots of the equation  The simplest way to factor a quadratic with a leading coefficient of 1 is to find two numbers that multiply to $c$ and add up to $b$. These numbers are then used to factor the quadratic as $(x + p)(x + q)$

## Completing Square Formula

To complete the square for a quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

1. Ensure $a = 1$ (if not, divide by $a$).
2. Move $c$ to the right side.
3. Add and subtract $\left(\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2$ to the left side.
4. Factor the left side into a perfect square trinomial.
5. Solve using the square root property.

Formula: 
$$ax^2 + bx + c = a\left(x + \frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 + \left(c - \frac{b^2}{4a}\right)$$
