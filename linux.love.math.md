To solve these problems, we can use the formula: 
$$Total\_Cost = P \* R \* I \* N \* T$$ 
where:
- P is the principal amount (the initial cost)
- R is the rate (in this case, it's not an interest rate, but rather the number of items or years)
- I is not applicable in these scenarios as there's no interest
- N is not applicable in these scenarios as there's no number of times interest is compounded
- T is the time, which in some cases is the number of years

However, a more straightforward approach for these specific problems is to simply multiply the cost by the number of items or years. Let's solve each problem step by step:

1. **Ubuntu Advantage Subscription**: 
The cost is $150 per year for 3 years. 
$$Total\_Cost = 150 \* 3 = 450$$

2. **Red Hat Enterprise Linux Licenses**: 
The cost is $349 per year for 5 licenses. 
$$Total\_Cost = 349 \* 5 = 1745$$

3. **Debian Project Donation**: 
The donation is $25 per month for 2 years. First, calculate the annual donation: 
$$Annual\_Donation = 25 \* 12 = 300$$
Then, calculate the total donation for 2 years: 
$$Total\_Amount = 300 \* 2 = 600$$

4. **CentOS Server Support**: 
The cost is $1,200 per year per server for 8 servers. 
$$Total\_Cost = 1200 \* 8 = 9600$$

5. **Linux Certification Course**: 
The course costs $200, and there's an annual subscription fee of $50. 
$$Total\_Cost = 200 + 50 = 250$$

6. **Linux-Based Security Solution**: 
The cost is $500 per year for 3 years. 
$$Total\_Cost = 500 \* 3 = 1500$$

7. **Linux Mint Premium Version**: 
The cost is $30 per year for 5 years. 
$$Total\_Cost = 30 \* 5 = 150$$

8. **SUSE Linux Enterprise Licenses**: 
The cost is $699 per year for 10 licenses. 
$$Total\_Cost = 699 \* 10 = 6990$$

So, the total costs for each scenario are calculated directly by multiplying the annual cost by the number of years or the number of licenses needed.
